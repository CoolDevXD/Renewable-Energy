What is Renewable Energy?

Renewable energy is energy that comes from natural resources like sunlight, wind, rain, and geothermal heat. These resources are naturally replenished and provide a clean, sustainable alternative to fossil fuels.

Why Renewable Energy Matters

Cleaner Air

Switching to renewable energy reduces harmful emissions, leading to better air quality and healthier lives.

Preserves Resources

Renewable energy helps conserve limited natural resources like coal, oil, and natural gas.

Combats Climate Change

By reducing greenhouse gas emissions, renewable energy is crucial in fighting global warming.

Types of Renewable Energy

Solar Energy

Solar panels capture sunlight and convert it into electricity. This is one of the cleanest energy sources.

Wind Energy

Wind turbines use wind to generate electricity, providing a sustainable energy source for millions.

Hydropower

Hydropower uses water flow to generate electricity, making it a renewable energy giant.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy uses heat from beneath the Earth's surface to generate power and provide heating.

How You Can Help

  1. Install solar panels to generate your own electricity.
  2. Choose an energy provider that uses renewable sources.
  3. Switch to energy-efficient appliances and LED lights.
  4. Reduce energy waste by unplugging devices when not in use.
  5. Encourage your community to invest in renewable energy projects.
